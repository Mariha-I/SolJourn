Think of it like Pokemon Go but for stars!! SolJourn is meant to be like a handheld personal telescope, a powerful little guide to stargazing for people who have never done it before.

What it does right now:
- takes data from the HYG database and plots it in real space to help you get a sense of what stars are arouund you, even when the weather isn't optimal for viewing
  - Comverts right ascension and declination data into alt and azimuth coordinates
- responds to the location and tilt of your phone/device showing you stars in the field of view of your camera lens
- lets you zoom in on the supposed position of stars to help you locate them in real space more easily

What's coming next:
- More intuitive interface: highlight select stars and constellations, display other celestial bodies as well, display names of stars/constellations/celestial bodies
- Make the star map an overlay of the actual camera: allow users to toggle between the star map and their actual camera through the app to help take fire pictures
  - to that end, integrate camera filters and enhancements to give users crisper shots
- Set up the project with Unity Version Control
